01 · The Story

A hand-painted identity,
built to last digitally.

M&N Guest House is a welcoming accommodation on Caye Caulker — one of Belize's most beloved island destinations, known for its calm turquoise waters, unhurried pace, and its famous motto: "Go Slow." The guest house had already built a reputation through word of mouth and a loyal returning clientele.

The brand, however, lived entirely on a hand-painted sign. There were no digital files — no logo the owners could send to a printer, drop onto a Facebook page, or use when listing the property on booking platforms. The identity existed only in the physical world, and the physical world alone.

The brief was precise: don't reimagine the brand — preserve it. Take what was painted by hand with care and character, and recreate it digitally with the same level of craft. Every letter, every proportion, every visual element faithfully reconstructed for the modern platforms a thriving guest house needs.

🖌️
No Digital Assets — Only a Physical Sign

Without source files or original artwork to work from, the only reference was the painted sign itself. Fonts were identified by eye, elements were traced, and the entire mark was rebuilt vector-by-vector.

02 · The Challenge

Three problems.
One solution.

Recreating a hand-painted logo without source files demands a specific combination of technical skill, artistic sensitivity, and patience. Here's what we were working against.

🔍
No Source Files

The original logo had never been created digitally. There were no AI, EPS, or even PDF files to work from — only a photograph of the hand-painted sign. Every element had to be identified, analyzed, and rebuilt from scratch.

🔤
Font Identification

Matching typefaces to hand-painted lettering is a precise craft. The letterforms were studied carefully — examining weight, width, and distinctive character shapes — until matching fonts were identified and confirmed against the original artwork.

✏️
Manual Tracing & Accuracy

Visual elements that couldn't be matched to existing fonts or assets were manually traced and drawn — preserving the unique character of the original while producing clean, scalable vector paths that perform at any size.

04 · Technique & Deliverables

Precision work for
a Caribbean landmark.

Analog soul,
digital precision.

Vectorization work of this kind sits at the intersection of technical skill and artistic judgment. It's not just tracing — it's understanding what made the original compelling and making sure that quality transfers fully to the new medium.

For M&N Guest House, that meant carefully studying the painted letterforms, isolating each font used in the composition, matching them against thousands of typeface options, and rebuilding every element as clean, editable vector paths.

Where exact font matches couldn't be found, components were manually redrawn — curve by curve — ensuring the final file looks exactly as it should while giving the client full ownership of a professional, editable design asset they can use forever.

05 · Process

How a painted sign
becomes a digital brand.

No shortcuts. Just careful, methodical work — the same four steps that guide every Visual Graphics project, applied here with the detail this kind of preservation demands.

01
Analyze

We studied every element of the original sign — letterforms, proportions, visual marks, and color. Understanding what existed before building anything new was the only path to accuracy.

02
Identify

Typefaces were matched letter-by-letter against thousands of options until exact or near-exact matches were confirmed. Elements that couldn't be matched were flagged for manual tracing.

03
Reconstruct

Every element was rebuilt as clean vector paths — using matched fonts for typography and manual Bézier drawing for custom marks — until the digital version stood side-by-side with the original.

04
Deliver

Final files packaged and delivered in every format needed — SVG, PNG, PDF, and all background variants — ready for immediate use across digital and print platforms.
