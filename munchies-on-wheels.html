01 · The Story

From After Hours
to a brand on wheels.

Munchies on Wheels started as "After Hour Munchies" — a name that described exactly what it was: hot food for people spilling out of the nightclub on Caye Caulker at midnight. The food was good. The brand wasn't there yet.

The owner was ready to level up. A new trailer, a proper setup, and the ambition to eventually go beyond late-night — adding lunch hours and building a business that could stand on its own outside of the club's schedule. All it needed was a brand that matched that ambition.

Our job was to give Munchies on Wheels an identity that would look right under neon lights at midnight and in the midday sun — energetic, bold, and impossible to scroll past on the feed.

The trailer wrap was the main event. A full vehicle wrap transforms a plain trailer into a landmark — and on an island the size of Caye Caulker, a landmark gets seen by everyone, every day.

We designed the wrap to do two jobs: reinforce the brand identity and function as signage. The menu panels were integrated directly into the wrap design, so the trailer sells itself without a separate board.

06 · Process

How we built it —
from brief to the street.

The Visual Graphics process, same four steps every time. The difference is the details — and the details here were very orange.

01
Discover

Understanding the business: late-night crowd, the nightclub context, the rebrand from After Hour Munchies, and the long-term goal of adding lunch hours.

02
Concept

Multiple logo directions explored — bold wordmarks, icon-forward marks, and hybrid lockups. The chosen direction led with appetite and attitude.

03
Refine

Tested the logo on the trailer template before anything was printed. Refined proportions, type weights, and color contrast until it was print-ready.

04
Deliver

Final files for the wrap printer, menu panels, social use, and future expansion — including the lunch pivot materials, prepped and waiting.
