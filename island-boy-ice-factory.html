01 · The Brief

Heritage on the rocks.

Island Boy Ice Factory is a wholesale & retail ice business in Caye Caulker — a sandy strip of Belize where the lobster traps outnumber the lampposts. The owner came to us with one ask: a brand that respected the work behind it, and the island it came from.

No clip-art penguins. No corporate snowflakes. Something that could sit on a 40lb ice bag headed to a restaurant kitchen, on a delivery cart, and on the side of a cooler at a roadside bar — and feel equally at home in every one of those places.

The result had to travel: from a freezer shelf to a ferry dock. From a business card to the side of a golf cart. Legible at fifty feet, sharp at five inches.

02 · Brand DNA

Three pillars carved
from the same block.

01
🌴
Rooted

The lobster — a daily reality on Caye Caulker — anchors the mark in place. It's not decoration, it's a passport. Every element of the brand traces back to island identity.

02
📦
Reliable

The ice box silhouette is steady, square, dependable. Restaurants run on the assumption their ice will arrive fresh and on time. The brand reflects that contract.

03
👁
Recognizable

Bold typography and high-contrast color make the brand legible across a bag of ice in a freezer or on the back of a delivery cart at full speed down Caye Caulker's main strip.

03 · The Mark

A logo that reads
at fifty feet
and on a sticker.

Two icons, one story. The ice box says the job. The lobster says the island. The serif and the slab together carry weight — like a hand-painted sign that's been there longer than you have.

🧊
The Ice Box

Speaks directly to the trade — wholesale, retail, cold chain. Square, dependable. The first thing a restaurant buyer should feel is trust.

🦞
The Lobster

Owner's heritage. A direct nod to Caye Caulker's fishing community and what makes the island run. It earns the word "Island" in the name.

🔤
The Type Lockup

Bold display paired with condensed support — designed to scale from a sticker to signage without losing presence or legibility.

🎯
The Principle

Every element earns its place. Nothing is decoration. If it doesn't reinforce the business or the island, it doesn't ship.

05 · Process

From a frozen brief
to a brand that ships.

The Visual Graphics process — same four steps for every project, whether it's an ice factory or a fashion boutique.

01
Discover

Conversations with the owner. Walks past the existing setup. Photos of the ice bags, the golf cart, the freezer — finding what was already working and what wasn't.

02
Concept

Three directions: literal (the box), heritage (the lobster), and a hybrid lockup combining both stories. The third direction was always going to win.

03
Refine

We tuned proportions, tested it small (sticker), large (signage), and on a real ice bag mockup — until every weight and curve felt earned.

04
Deliver

Final files in every format the business needs — SVG, PNG, PDF, mono versions, social cuts. Ready for the bag printer, the sign shop, and the feed.
