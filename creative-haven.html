01 · The Story

A business with real demand
and no brand to show for it.

Creative Haven had already built something impressive: a growing base of customers ordering custom cups, engraved keychains, printed bags, and personalized party items — all without heavy marketing. The demand was there. The product was there. The brand wasn't.

With orders coming in and a reputation building by word of mouth, the business needed a logo that could keep up — something professional, flexible, and confident enough to live on every surface they engrave or print.

The challenge: design a mark that felt creative and warm without being chaotic, and that could scale cleanly from a tiny keychain to a full-size printed banner.

🎨
Identity Before Marketing: The Right Order of Operations

A business with real traction needed a brand that matched. We designed the logo first — so every future campaign, sticker, or product already starts from a strong foundation.

Logo works across all product surfaces

A logo tourists recognize. A brand that closes every custom order.

The design process started with the product range. Creative Haven makes a huge variety of items — and the logo had to feel at home on all of them without being retrofitted. That meant simplicity, flexibility, and color confidence.

We focused on a mark that was friendly and approachable (because customers are buying personalized gifts and celebrations) while still feeling professional enough to build lasting business trust.

The result: a logo that's as comfortable on a laser-engraved acrylic keychain as it is on an Instagram profile. Clean at 16px, striking at full bleed.

05 · Process

From brief to
brand-ready files.

A focused four-step design process that turned a business need into a complete, production-ready brand identity.

01
Discover

We explored the products, the customers, and the tone the business needed to project — cheerful but professional, creative but reliable. That brief became the design brief.

02
Concept

Color direction and mark concepts were explored with the product range in mind. Every choice was tested against the core question: does this work on a keychain? A bag? A website?

03
Refine

The chosen direction was refined until the mark felt confident at every size — from a 16px favicon to a full-bleed print, the logo had to hold its clarity and character throughout.

04
Deliver

Final files delivered in SVG, PDF, and PNG — ready for laser engravers, digital platforms, printers, and social media profiles. A complete brand kit, production-ready on day one.
